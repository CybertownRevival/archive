<p align="center"><font color="#33FF00" size="4" face="Arial"><strong>SAMPLE BLOCK REPORTS</strong></font></p>
<p align="left"> </p>
<p align="left"><font face="Arial">Although verbal communication between staff already happens, often
 something in </font>
<font color="#EAE7A6" face="Arial">written form can be a quick reminder or reference</font>
<font face="Arial"> when needed. They are an optional tool that can assist staff in their communication.</font></p>
<p align="left"><font face="Arial">Whether or not BL's use such a report is </font>
<font color="#EAE7A6" face="Arial">up to the NL</font>
<font face="Arial">, as is the timing of submission and format. BL's could email their report to their
 assigned ND or upload it as a webpage, ideally perhaps every 2 weeks (maybe up to 4 weeks).</font></p>
<p align="left"><font face="Arial">A </font>
<font color="#EAE7A6" face="Arial">sample block report </font>
<font face="Arial">is provided below and can be used, or modified, for BL's to report on </font>
<font color="#EAE7A6" face="Arial">how their block is progressing.</font></p>
<p align="left"><font face="Arial">Also provided is a </font>
<font color="#EAE7A6" face="Arial">sample responsibility list</font>
<font face="Arial"> that shows the </font>
<font color="#EAE7A6" face="Arial">assignment of tasks</font>
<font face="Arial"> to BL's & BD's . Although often most share the tasks, to actually have one person
 <u>responsible</u> for a task, minimizes the "thinking someone else is doing it and hence no one does"
  possibility. Plus, also fosters the delegation and sharing of duties hence increasing BD participation & training.</font></p>
