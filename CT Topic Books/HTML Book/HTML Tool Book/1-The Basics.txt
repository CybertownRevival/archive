<font color=skyblue size=3><P>HTML Basics</p></font>
<font color=white>*Brackets*</font><br>
<font color=coral>All HTML tags are enclosed within</font>
<font color=yellow><  ></font>
<font color=coral> brackets. Anything that is written between the</font>
<font color=yellow><   ></font>
<font color=coral>brackets will be invisible, and not appear on your page.</font></p>
<P></P><br><font color=white>*Case Sensitive*</font><br>
<font color=coral>HTML tags are NOT case sensitive. So,</font>
<font color=yellow>< center ></font>
<font color=coral>is also the same as</font>
<font color=yellow>< CENTER ></font>
<font color=coral>is also the same as</font>
<font color=yellow>< Center >.</font></p>
<P><font color=lime>EXCEPT: image filenames! ... the filename should be written exactly as it appears. Example:</font>
<font color= skyblue>< img src=(url)"spookie.gif" ></font>
<font color=coral>is not the same as</font>
<font color=skyblue>< img src=(url) "spookie.GIF" ></font></p>
<P></P><br><font color=white>*Opening & Closing Tags*</font>
<br><font color=coral>Almost all HTML commands have an opening and a closing tag. For example, the opening
 tag,</font>
<font color=yellow>< center ></font>
<font color=coral>causes all text and graphics that appear after the tag to be centered in the page. 
Everything will remain centered until the closing tag</font>
<font color=yellow>< /center ></font>
<font color=coral> is used. The</font>
<font color=yellow> / </font>
<font color=coral>is the command to close that particular function.</font></p>
