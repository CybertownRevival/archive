<font color=white size=3>*HORIZONTAL LINES*</font>
<br><font color=yellow><br>< hr ></font>
<font color=coral>This tag makes a very fine shaded horizontal line. NO closing tag is needed.</font></p> 
<p><font color=coral>You can also control the thickness, length and alignment of the line, by combining the 
following attributes: </font><br>
<font color=coral>Width is expressed as a</font>
<font color=yellow> % </font>
<font color=coral> of the width of the page. Example:</font>
<font color=yellow>< hr width=50% ></font>
<hr width=50%></p>
<font color=lime>
<p>--------------------------------------------------------------------------------</p></font>
<P></P><font color=skyblue>Size:</font>
<font color=coral> is (thickness) expressed as a number (of pixels). For example,</font>
<font color=yellow>< hr width=50% size10 ></font>
<hr width=50% size=10>
<font color=lime>
<P>--------------------------------------------------------------------------------</p></font>
<P></P><font color=skyblue>Align:</font>
<font color=coral>can be left or right. If not specified, it will default to the center.</font><br>
<font color=coral>Example:</font>
<font color=yellow>< hr width=50% size 10 align right ></font>
<hr width=50% size=10 align=right>
<font color=lime>
<P>--------------------------------------------------------------------------------</p></font>
<P></P><font color=skyblue>Noshade:</font>
<font color=coral>You can make the line dark by specifying noshade.</font><BR>
<font color=coral>Example:</font>
<font color=yellow>< hr width=50% size 10 align right noshade ></font>
<hr width=50% size=10 align=right noshade></td>
